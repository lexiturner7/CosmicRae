What makes an asteroid potentially hazardous?

NASA classifies an asteroid as "Potentially Hazardous" based on two things: size and orbit.
It must be larger than about 140 meters AND its orbit must bring it within about 7.5 million kilometers of Earth. That's roughly 20 times the distance to the Moon.
So, an asteroid can pass closer to Earth but still be "safe" if it's small, while a larger asteroid further away might be flagged as hazardous because of how its orbit is shaped. It doesn't mean it will hit Earth, just that it's worth watching closely!
